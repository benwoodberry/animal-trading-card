Capybara

The capybara's eyes, ears and nose all rest on the upper half of their heads so they can still see while submerged in water, much like the hippopotamus.

  • Scientific Name: Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris
  • Average Length: 3.2 to 4.2 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 4 to 8 years
  • Habitat: Bodies of water in savannas and dense forests

Heavy and barrel-shaped with short legs, the capybara is a semi-aquatic rodent with physical features that suit its environment.They are known to be strong swimmers, and their webbed feet help them propel through water. They can weigh up to 150 pounds, but this and their build don't prevent them from jumping high and reaching land speeds of 20 miles per hour!
